Mount Khuiten is one of the highest peaks of the Altai Mountains and the highest point of Mongolia at 4374 m. The Kuithen Peak simultaneously borders Russia, China, and Kazakhstan and is close to other beautiful peaks: Malchin, Niaramdal, Naran, Snowchurch, and Olgii, and Mongolia’s longest glacier, Potanin, 19 km long.

Fly Fishing

date_range

Travel Time: June 15th - October 15th

Transportation: Toyota Land Cruiser

Group Size: 2 - 10 people

access_time

Duration: 11 days

The Khoton and Khurgan Lakes are the second largest and clearest of all lakes in Mongolia. The average depth is 26 meters; the maximum depth is approximately 58 meters. The landscape around the lakes is stunning: with forests and the snow-capped Altai Mountains. Khoton and Khurgan Lakes are home to the grayling (thymallus arcticus) and the lenok (brachymystax lenok).The lakes are located in an intermontane tectonic depression in the northern portion of the Mongolian Altai Region. The lakes themselves, as well as the mountains and rivers which feed into the lakes, offer excellent sport fishing in a rugged and virtually unpopulated area.
